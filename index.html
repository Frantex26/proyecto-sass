The Dark Souls trilogy, and its related games

What is it?

Dark Souls trilogy is an action role-playing video game. Developed by FromSoftware and distributed by Namco Bandai Games. Dark Souls trilogy is a series of games that covers Dark Souls, Dark Souls II and Dark Souls III. Its a third-person action role-playing game. One of the core mechanic of the game is exploration. Players are encouraged to proceed with caution, learn from past mistakes, or find alternative areas to explore. Dark Souls takes place in a large and continuous, interconnected environment, connected through a central hub area. The player character can travel between areas and explore various paths at will, although prerequisites must be met to unlock certain areas. The Dark Souls Trilogy began with the release of Dark Souls in October 2011 worldwide for the PlayStation and Xbox consoles. The PC version was released almost a year later in August 2012. The great success achieved in his first game gave rise to his second installment called Dark Souls II, which was released worldwide in March 2014, with great acceptance by the players when they saw the new mechanics that it included. To culminate his latest installment of the trilogy called Dark Souls III which was released worldwide in April 2016, FromSoftware implemented a much better image quality than its previous games, as well as an improved combat system and a lot of other qualities that they made this one of the best games to date. The Dark Souls trilogy was awarded multiple times for its innovative gameplay as well as its incredible high difficulty. All Dark Souls Trilogy won Game of the Year at the Golden Joystick Awards.

Gameplay

Dark Souls focuses on dungeon exploring and the tension and fear that arise when players encounter enemies in this setting. The player character's interaction with the world of Dark Souls includes a great deal of combat. Combat includes melee attacks, various forms of defensive maneuvering, and magical abilities. For melee attacks, player characters have access to a wide range of medieval-style weaponryâ€”including swords, spears, maces, magical swords, huge greatswords, and immense clubs. For defence, player characters have access to shields, armour, dodging, and parrying. For magic, which includes a wide variety of offensive and defensive abilities known as sorceries, miracles, and pyromancies. The player can learn these different skills through his journey through the world, either by buying them in stores that offer a wide variety of equipment, consumables and of course the skills already mentioned, or on the other hand by finding them around the world in the form of an object. These various forms of combat all have tradeoffs, variations, and in-game costs associated with them. They can all be buffed or altered in some fashion through levelling, smithing, consumable items, magical rings, and new equipment. Souls are the universal currency of the Dark Souls trilogy. They are used to level up, buy items, upgrade/upgrade weapons, upgrade armor, and repair equipment. Souls are earned by slaying enemies, which is the best way to get them, the other way is by slaying bosses which give you a large amount of souls, or by consuming items that give you a set number of souls.

Bonfire

A Bonfire is a typical resting station in Dark Souls trilogy and a crucial gameplay element. Bonfires are located throughout the world and they are almost always safe enemy-free areas, although there are exceptions. When a player rests at a bonfire, their health and stamina refill, along with their spells and Estus Flask, and all negative effects on the player (bleed, poison and toxic) are removed except Curse. Players may not rest at a bonfire if there are enemies nearby. From the bonfire, a player can perform many tasks. Upon losing all of their health points and dying, players lose their Souls and are teleported back to a bonfire where they last rested, which serves as a checkpoint. One chance is given for the player to recover their lost Souls in the form of a bloodstain, which is placed at or around where they last died. If the player dies again before reaching their bloodstain, the Souls are permanently gone. As most enemies are respawned following player death, or if the player rest at a bonfire, the player has the opportunity to regain more Souls by repeated combat encounters. The bonfire is a type of campfire in the action role-playing game Dark Souls and its sequels, that functions as a checkpoint for the player character's progress, as well as reviving most enemies that the player previously killed. Later in the game, and in Dark Souls II and III, they function as warp points.

Multiplayer

Online interaction in the Souls games is integrated into the single-player experience. Throughout levels, players can briefly see the actions of other players as ghosts in the same area that may show hidden passages or switches. When a player dies, a bloodstain can be left in other players' game world that when activated can show a ghost playing out their final moments, indicating how that person died and potentially helping the player avoid the same fate in advance. Players can leave messages on the ground that can either help other players by providing hints and warnings or harm them by leaving false hints. Players can also engage in both player versus player combat (PVP) and cooperative gameplay using invasion or summoning mechanics. The PVP experience varies depending on what is chosen, on the one hand we have a section of the game in which we can choose to enter the PVP, which will match us against another player with a similar level and we will battle to see who is the best. On the other hand we have the terror of the players, since there is a mechanism that allows players to invade other players' worlds, this means that if you are traveling around the world enjoying the game, it is possible that another player invades you and attack, this is known as invading; And the only way to make the other player go away is to kill him or get killed. All this in real time, which makes the battles more exciting. Cooperative mode lets two players connect simultaneously to help each other to pass a zone or beat a boss. This allows you to connect with a friend through a password or connect with a stranger through an item that leaves a help signal on the ground, which other players can see and use if they choose to help you.
